What's in the pack

Two map layers, built from the same data behind the rest of this site. See Methodology for how events are detected and rated.

  • Traffic Density (blue/purple) — where the airplanes actually are. Aggregated position reports over the whole data range; the brighter the area, the busier the airspace.
  • LOS Events — individual loss-of-separation events, colored by confidence: very high, high, medium. Low-confidence events are excluded.

Installing

On an iPad or iPhone — press and hold the Install in ForeFlight button above (don't just tap it), then choose Open in ForeFlight. ForeFlight downloads and imports the pack for you. This only works on a device with ForeFlight installed: the button is a handoff to the app, not a web page, so on a computer it just leads to a ForeFlight error page. Use Download .zip there instead.

From a Mac

  • AirDrop: download the .zip, then in Finder right-click it → Share → AirDrop → your iPad. Accept on the iPad and choose ForeFlight.
  • USB: connect the iPad, open it in Finder, click Files, and drag the .zip onto ForeFlight.

Other ways

  • Email: mail the .zip to yourself, open it on the iPad, press and hold the attachment, and choose Copy to ForeFlight.
  • Safari: download the .zip on the iPad, open the Files app, press and hold the file, then Share → ForeFlight.

Turning the layers on

Once imported, the pack appears under More → Custom Content. Its two layers then show up in the Maps layer selector, in the left-hand column — switch them on there. Remove or replace the pack from the same Custom Content screen.

Please note

These layers are a historical picture of a fixed date range. They are not live traffic, not a traffic advisory, and no substitute for see-and-avoid or ATC. They will not update in flight. Events are inferred from crowdsourced ADS-B data and any individual one may be a false positive — see Methodology for the known sources of error.
